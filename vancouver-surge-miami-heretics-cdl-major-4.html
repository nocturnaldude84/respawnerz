Vancouver Surge Sweep Miami Heretics in CDL Major IV Qualifiers

Nero led the way with 8,948 damage and a 2.75 K/D on Search and Destroy as Vancouver Surge closed out a 3-0 win over Miami Heretics in week three of the Major IV online qualifiers.

June 21, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 6 min read

Vancouver Surge swept Miami Heretics 3-0 on Sunday in week three of the Call of Duty League Major IV online qualifiers, picking up a result that matters more than the scoreline alone suggests. With the Major IV LAN set for Paris La Defense Arena from June 26 to 28, every qualifier match is shaping final seeding, and both Surge and Heretics came into Sunday sitting in the bottom half of the standings.

The win was carried in large part by Nero, who finished the series with 8,948 total damage, a 1.32 K/D across the full set, and a standout 2.75 K/D on Search and Destroy specifically. His Hardpoint K/D came in at 1.12 and his Overload K/D at 1.30, giving Surge a consistent damage threat across all three game modes that make up a CDL series this season.

Why This Match Matters Beyond the Scoreline

Heading into Sunday, Miami Heretics sat ninth in the Major IV qualifier standings, with Vancouver Surge down in eleventh. Neither team has had a smooth run through Stage 4. Surge in particular have been through a rocky stretch, parting ways with TJHaLy in late May as part of a broader roster shake-up heading into this qualifier window, with Nero brought in to help stabilize the lineup alongside Craze, Lunarz, and Mamba.

A clean 3-0 sweep is exactly the kind of result a team in Surge's position needs. CDL Points earned during qualifier play carry directly into seeding for the LAN event in Paris, and every match win matters when a team is fighting to climb out of the bottom tier of a 12-team field. For Heretics, the loss is a setback at a point in the qualifier window where there is very little room left to recover ground.

A Long Season Building to Paris

This result is part of the lead-up to Major IV, the fourth and final regular-season Major of the 2026 Call of Duty League calendar before the season transitions into the playoffs in July. Unlike the first three Majors, Major IV is being hosted overseas, marking Call of Duty Black Ops 7 competitive play's first stop in Paris and giving European fans a rare chance to watch the league live.

All 12 CDL franchises are competing across this qualifier window, with matches played across Hardpoint, Search and Destroy, and Overload, the newest mode introduced for the 2026 season. Standings built through these qualifier weeks determine who lands in the winners bracket versus the elimination bracket once the format moves offline, which is exactly why a result like Surge's sweep over Heretics carries weight well beyond a single Sunday scoreline.

What Comes Next

With qualifier play wrapping up before the Paris LAN begins, every remaining match in Stage 4 functions as a final chance for teams in the middle and lower tiers of the standings to improve their seed. For Vancouver Surge, Sunday's sweep is a step in the right direction after a turbulent month of roster changes. For Miami Heretics, the result puts more pressure on the matches that remain before Major IV gets underway in France.
