GTA 6 Ultimate Edition: Every Vehicle, Weapon, and Bonus Included

Rockstar dropped the full Ultimate Edition breakdown just hours ahead of pre-orders going live on June 25, and it's a lot more than a few paint jobs. Six vehicles, engraved sidearms, five exclusive shops, and a separate Vintage Vice City Pack on top of it. Here's everything confirmed so far, and what's still just speculation.

June 24, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 6 min read

Rockstar waited almost until the last minute on this one. With pre-orders for Grand Theft Auto VI set to go live at midnight local time on June 25, the studio used the hours beforehand to finally explain what the $99.99 Ultimate Edition actually gets you. Short version: it's cosmetic, it's substantial, and you won't have all of it the moment you load the game.

It Unlocks As You Play, Not All At Once

One thing worth knowing before you decide which edition to put money down on: none of this content is handed to you on day one. Rockstar has confirmed that Ultimate Edition items, vehicles, shops, the works, unlock progressively as Jason and Lucia work through each chapter of the story. So if you were picturing a garage full of bonus cars the second the title screen loads, that's not how it's structured.

Six Vehicles, Including a Boat With Its Own Weapons Stash

The vehicle list leans heavily on Jason's side of the story. There's a '95 Grotti Cheetah done up in a retro-futuristic livery, a green Dinka Enduro dirt bike parked at his safehouse, and an orange Crest Kayak sitting right alongside it. His Vapid Ganado gets an exclusive Retro Build modkit, and a '67 Vapid Dominator Buggy comes stored at the Paradise Garage in Watson Bay, which doubles as a weapons locker and a place to stash stolen goods. The standout might be the Shitzu Squalo, a pink-and-blue watercraft docked at Washington Beach that ships with its own crate of explosives.

Engraved Sidearms for Both Leads

Weapons get the same custom treatment. Jason and Lucia each get a Hawk & Little Morgan Revolver styled after the Vercetti Estate, complete with a scope and palm-tree grips, a nice nod for anyone who remembers Vice City. On top of that, Jason gets a personalized Girardi ES9 and Lucia gets her own engraved Klose K17 pistol.

Five Shops You Can't Visit Any Other Way

This is where the Ultimate Edition does most of its heavy lifting. Rideout Customs Mod Shop handles interiors, rims, and donk-style builds for regular vehicles. Sara's Unisex Hair Salon covers new hairstyles for both characters, plus facial hair for Jason and makeup and nails for Lucia. Stock 305 in Stockyard is a standalone clothing store with its own line of streetwear, and the Electric Fang Tattoo Shop next door offers more than fifty tattoo designs created by the artist collective FAILE. Out by Lake Leonida, One-Eyed Willie's Mod Shop rounds things out with off-road builds and hand-painted finishes.

Two Side Hustles Tied to the Edition

Beyond the cosmetics, Ultimate Edition owners get two extra activities. The PTT Youngin$ Illegal Goods Store is a raidable compound belonging to one of Southside Vice City's more active gangs, good for unique contraband if you're willing to go in loud. There's also a Classic Car Collection Special Commission, where a fixer named Wyman sends you hunting for abandoned classic and unfinished project cars to restore.

Two Wardrobes: Vice City Style and Goodtime Gear

Apparel comes in two themed sets. Vice City Style adds exclusive outfits and tattoos pulled from that era's aesthetic. Goodtime Gear is the weirder one, a line of clothing and accessories built around Macca the Gator, the in-universe TV mascot from the fictional Goodtime State.

Don't Confuse This With the Vintage Vice City Pack

One thing that's tripping people up: the Vintage Vice City Pack is not part of the Ultimate Edition. It's a separate pre-order bonus that comes with either edition, Standard or Ultimate, as long as you pre-order before launch. Buying Ultimate doesn't replace it; you're getting both if you pre-order the Ultimate Edition specifically. Rockstar leans hard into the nostalgia angle for this one, going as far as to describe it as a welcome back to the era when the neon burned brightest.

The centerpiece is a '55 Vapid Stanier sedan, parked in a personal garage called Shore Court just off Ocean Beach. Like the Ultimate Edition's garages, it comes with its own weapon locker and a secure spot to offload stolen goods. On the wardrobe side, Jason gets a linen suit in a vintage pastel shade with a matching haircut, while Lucia gets a red sequin mini dress and curls to go with it, both clearly built to evoke the look of the original Vice City era rather than anything modern. The pack also throws in a weapon pattern Rockstar is calling "Channel the Original Kingpin," a tropical print that mirrors Tommy Vercetti's palm-tree shirt and can be applied to most guns in the game.

Price and Release Date

Grand Theft Auto VI launches November 19, 2026, on PS5 and Xbox Series X|S. Pre-orders open everywhere, digital storefronts and select retailers, at midnight local time on June 25. As of this writing, Rockstar's own site does not list a price for either edition, so anything circulating right now, including the $79.99 Standard and $99.99 Ultimate figures making the rounds, is unconfirmed. Treat those as speculation until Rockstar or a storefront posts an official number. Full details on the Ultimate Edition and the official screenshots are up now at rockstargames.com/VI.

Cosmetics that drip-feed over a single-player campaign are a reasonable ask, less reasonable if Rockstar's idea of "progress through the story" turns out to mean the back third of the game. We won't know that, or what either edition actually costs, until Rockstar says so.
