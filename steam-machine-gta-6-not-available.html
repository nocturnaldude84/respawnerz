Valve's New $1,049 Steam Machine Can't Play GTA 6 At Launch, And That's A Bigger Problem Than You Think

The Steam Machine ships June 30. GTA 6 ships November 19. Those two dates should have been a perfect match. Instead, Rockstar has confirmed the game is console-only at launch, and the history of how long PC waits for GTA says you probably won't be playing Vice City on your Steam Machine in 2026.

June 23, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 9 min read

Two of the biggest gaming releases of 2026 are about to miss each other completely, and the people in the middle are the ones who just spent over a thousand dollars on Valve's new box.

The Steam Machine starts shipping June 30. GTA 6 launches November 19, exclusively on PS5 and Xbox Series X/S. Rockstar has not announced a PC version, has not published PC system requirements, and has not given any public indication of when PC players will get access. Based on what the studio did with GTA V, the wait could stretch well past 2027.

For anyone buying a Steam Machine specifically to play the biggest game of the year on their TV, that is a serious problem. Not a dealbreaker for everyone, but a problem. And when your device costs $1,049 before you even add a controller, the stakes of that problem are worth thinking through clearly.

What the Steam Machine Actually Is

In case you have been following the price drama but not the hardware breakdown: the Steam Machine is a compact gaming PC built around a semi-custom AMD Zen 4 chip, an RDNA 3 GPU running at 110W, 16GB of DDR5 system memory, and 8GB of dedicated GDDR6 for graphics. It runs SteamOS 3, connects to your TV via HDMI, fits in a six-inch cube, and ships with an optional Steam Controller that adds $79 to whichever configuration you pick.

Four configurations at launch:

512GB, no controller: $1,049 / £879
512GB, with controller: $1,128 / £938
2TB, no controller: $1,349 / £1,149
2TB, with controller: $1,428 / £1,208

The pitch is living room PC gaming without building a tower or dealing with Windows. The library is your entire Steam catalog. The catch, as PC Gamer's 62/100 launch review made clear, is that the price does not match the performance. At 1440p, Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora averages 21 fps. Cyberpunk 2077 with ray tracing on Medium hits around 28. Neither is playable by most standards.

And none of it matters for GTA 6 right now anyway, because GTA 6 is not coming to PC at launch.

GTA 6 Is Not Coming to PC at Launch

Let's be specific about what is confirmed and what is not.

Rockstar has officially confirmed Grand Theft Auto VI for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S on November 19, 2026. That is the complete list of announced platforms. No PC, no Nintendo, no last-gen consoles. The official Rockstar game page does not list a PC version. The official PlayStation Store listing does not mention a PC version. There are no system requirements for PC because there is no announced PC game to write requirements for.

Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick addressed PC directly in an interview and confirmed the delay is deliberate, not an oversight. The strategy is to serve the "core audience" first. In Rockstar's world, that means the PlayStation 5 install base and, to a lesser degree, Xbox Series X/S. PC comes after. How long after is not something Zelnick or anyone at Rockstar has said publicly.

Pre-orders open June 25, five months before the November launch. Those pre-orders are for PS5 and Xbox only. PC players do not have a pre-order option because there is no PC release to pre-order. That is the full picture as of today.

Why Rockstar Keeps Skipping PC at Launch

This is not new behavior. Rockstar has done this with every major GTA release, and the reason is straightforward: it works.

GTA V launched on PS3 and Xbox 360 in September 2013. The PC version came out April 14, 2015, roughly 19 months later. In those 19 months, Rockstar had already sold tens of millions of copies on console. Then the PC release generated another wave of revenue. Then the PS4/Xbox One version. Then PS5/Series X in 2022, nine years after the original launch. GTA V never really stopped making money because Rockstar kept finding new groups of players to charge for the same game.

GTA 6 is being set up for the exact same play. Console first, PC later, a separate announcement when the time is right. The PC version of GTA 6 is almost certainly in development. Former Rockstar developers have spoken to outlets suggesting PC is very much planned. But "planned" and "announced" are different things, and Rockstar knows that keeping PC as a separate announcement keeps the game in the news cycle for longer.

Red Dead Redemption 2 followed a similar path. Console launch in October 2018, PC launch in November 2019, 13 months later. That is the faster end of Rockstar's range. GTA historically runs longer. The gap between console and PC has never been less than a year for any mainline GTA title.

So this is not about the Steam Machine specifically. Rockstar is not skipping SteamOS or targeting Valve. The studio just does not do simultaneous console-PC releases for its biggest titles. The Steam Machine happens to be launching into that pattern at the worst possible moment.

Why This Matters More for Valve Than for Rockstar

Rockstar will be fine. GTA 6 is going to sell enormous numbers on PS5 regardless of what happens with Steam or PC. The game has been delayed twice, has generated years of hype, and has a pre-order window opening five days from now. November 19 is already circled on a lot of calendars, and none of those people need a Steam Machine to play it.

Valve's situation is different. The Steam Machine was supposed to be the device that proved living room PC gaming could compete with consoles. The timing of its launch was a real asset on paper. June 30, five months before GTA 6, gave Valve a window to sell the box as the hardware you would want ready when the biggest game of the year arrived. That pitch has now collapsed.

And the problem is not just one game. It is what one game represents. GTA 6 is the kind of release that drives hardware sales. People buy consoles for it. If the Steam Machine cannot offer a path to GTA 6 on day one, or even on day 100, it loses a significant chunk of its gravitational pull as a purchase decision.

PC Gamer's review already pointed out that the Steam Machine struggles to justify its price against Mini PCs running RTX 5060s for similar money. The competitive landscape is rough. Losing GTA 6 from the launch window does not make that math better.

When Could GTA 6 Actually Arrive on Steam Machine

Nobody knows. That is the honest answer. But the history gives a reasonable range to work with.

If Rockstar follows the GTA V timeline, a PC version arrives roughly 18 to 19 months after the console launch. November 2026 plus 18 months puts a PC release somewhere around May 2028. That is not a confirmed date. It is a pattern. Rockstar could be faster, especially given how much has changed in the PC gaming market since 2013. Some analysts and industry watchers have floated a late 2027 window as an optimistic scenario, pointing to the Red Dead Redemption 2 timeline as a precedent for a faster turnaround.

Former Rockstar developers speaking informally to outlets have suggested the studio wanted GTA 6 out before the end of the fiscal year that closes in March 2027, though those comments referred to the console version and it is unclear whether PC was included in that framing. Take-Two's official financial guidance documents do not reference a PC release in the current fiscal cycle at all.

The PCGamesN estimate puts the GTA 6 PC release likely arriving by the end of 2027. That aligns with the Red Dead 2 gap more than the GTA V gap. Either way, Steam Machine owners are probably not playing GTA 6 in 2026, and possibly not in most of 2027 either.

When the PC version does arrive, it will presumably run on SteamOS through Proton, the same compatibility layer that handles the rest of the Steam Machine's library. Whether GTA 6 runs well under Proton at launch is its own separate question, and one that will not have an answer until Rockstar actually ships it.

Should GTA Fans Buy a Steam Machine Anyway

That depends on what GTA 6 represents in your gaming plans versus what everything else does.

If GTA 6 is the main reason you were considering a Steam Machine, the math does not work right now. You would be paying over a thousand dollars for a box that cannot play the game you want for at least a year, probably longer. A PS5 costs less, plays GTA 6 on day one, and runs a native library without a Proton translation layer in the way. For GTA specifically, a console is still the faster and cheaper path.

If GTA 6 is one game among many, and you have a large Steam library and want a living room device to access it, the calculation is different. The Steam Machine does what it says. It runs your existing library, sits quietly under the TV, and Valve will presumably keep improving SteamOS over time. The performance ceiling is real, but for older or less demanding games it works fine. If you already own a thousand hours of games on Steam, the device has immediate value that has nothing to do with GTA 6's release schedule.

The harder question is whether that value justifies the price. PC Gamer's review is a 62 out of 100 for hardware that costs more than $1,000. For context, a mini PC with an RTX 5060 costs about $11 more than the 2TB Steam Machine bundle and beats it by a wide margin on benchmarks. If pure gaming performance per dollar is what you are optimizing for, there are better options.

What you lose with those alternatives is SteamOS. That matters to some people and not at all to others. Windows is not for everyone, and Valve has done real work making SteamOS a coherent living room experience. Whether that experience is worth the premium over a more powerful mini PC is genuinely a personal call.

The Bigger Picture

Valve is trying to do something genuinely difficult. Building a console replacement that competes on price, performance, and software ecosystem all at once was always going to be hard. The RAM shortage made it harder by pushing prices up before a single unit shipped. The PC Gamer review made it harder by publishing benchmarks that are tough to look at. And now GTA 6, the one game that could have helped justify the purchase to a much wider audience, is sitting on the other side of a wall that Rockstar controls and Valve cannot touch.

None of this is a death sentence for the Steam Machine. Hardware generations play out over years, not months. Prices for components will come down. SteamOS will improve. And at some point, probably in 2027 or 2028, GTA 6 will land on PC and run through Proton on every Steam Machine already out there.

But right now, today, the device launching June 30 cannot play the game launching November 19. That gap is real, and for anyone who was planning to use both as the centerpiece of their gaming setup this holiday season, it is worth being clear-eyed about what you are actually buying and when it will get the library you want.

Reservations for the Steam Machine are open now. The queue closes June 25, the same day GTA 6 pre-orders go live on PS5 and Xbox. That is a strange kind of symmetry for a situation that did not have to work out this way.
