GTA 6 Is Missing From PlayStation Stores in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman, But There's Still No Official Ban

With pre-orders now live, players across four Gulf countries are finding GTA VI absent from PlayStation Store search results entirely. Respawnerz checked all four storefronts and confirmed it. Nobody has officially explained why.

June 25, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 7 min read

Grand Theft Auto VI pre-orders went live today across PlayStation and Xbox digital storefronts, and most of the world can find the game without any trouble. Pull up the PlayStation Store, search GTA, there it is. Standard Edition, Ultimate Edition, your choice. That's not the experience players in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman are having right now. In those four storefronts, searching for Grand Theft Auto returns nothing from the new game. Not a listing. Not a placeholder page. Just the older Rockstar catalogue sitting there like it's 2013.

Respawnerz checked all four regional PlayStation Stores following reports that something looked off with availability in parts of the Middle East. The findings were consistent across the board. GTA VI, in any edition, does not appear in search results for Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait or Oman at the time of publication. What does show up is a familiar lineup of remasters and older titles: Grand Theft Auto: The Trilogy, The Definitive Edition, the standalone Vice City and San Andreas versions, Red Dead Redemption, and Red Dead Online. The new game is not among them.

Before anyone runs with the word "ban," that word has not been used by anyone with actual authority over this situation. Rockstar Games has not issued a statement. Sony Interactive Entertainment has not explained the missing listings. And no government body in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait or Oman has announced that GTA VI is prohibited. What exists right now is a gap between what players in those regions can find and what players everywhere else can access. The reason for that gap is still unknown.

Where the Confusion Started

The clearest public signal that something was different for these regions came from Sony's own Grand Theft Auto VI FAQ. The document covers a promotional offer tied to digital pre-orders: buy GTA VI through the PlayStation Store before launch and receive one free month of GTA+, Rockstar's subscription service for GTA Online.

Under the eligibility section, Sony lists specific PlayStation Store regions that are excluded from the promotion. The list includes Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman, alongside Lebanon, China, Russia and Taiwan. That's eight regions in total that won't receive the GTA+ month even if they complete a digital pre-order.

The FAQ is specifically about the promotional bonus. It doesn't say GTA VI won't be available in those countries. It doesn't say the game is banned. What it says is that those regions aren't getting the free GTA+ month. That's a meaningful distinction, but it was enough to kick off a wider conversation about whether players in those markets would be able to access the game at all. The missing store listings added fuel to that conversation.

What Respawnerz Found

We searched the official PlayStation Store for each of the four affected Gulf countries using both "Grand Theft Auto" and "GTA" as search terms.

All four searches returned the same pattern. The older Rockstar catalogue appeared normally. GTA VI did not appear in any form. There were no listings for the Standard Edition, no listings for the Ultimate Edition, and no coming-soon placeholder pages of the kind Sony sometimes uses for unreleased titles.

For comparison, PlayStation Stores in neighbouring regions where no issues have been reported show GTA VI listings with full pre-order functionality. The gap is specific to these four storefronts, at least based on what's visible to users searching for the game today.

We're not treating the missing listings as confirmation of a ban. There are multiple reasons a game might not appear in a regional storefront, and very few of them require an official prohibition to explain.

What Could Actually Be Happening

Regional storefront availability is more complicated than people tend to assume. A game going up for pre-order globally doesn't mean every regional PlayStation Store flips on at the same moment. Several things could explain why GTA VI isn't showing up in these four markets without a ban being involved.

Content rating approvals work differently across different markets. Some countries have regulatory bodies that assess video games before they can be listed for sale, and that process doesn't always line up with a global launch date. If those approvals haven't been completed in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait or Oman, Sony may be holding the listings back until they are.

It's also possible that Sony is deliberately delaying the listings while local review processes run in the background. That's not unusual for high-profile releases in markets with content assessment requirements. The game would still eventually become available once those processes conclude, with no ban ever having been announced.

A third possibility is that the storefront pages simply haven't been published yet in these regions. Administrative delays, regional publishing timelines, and behind-the-scenes localization work can all push a listing live later than the main global rollout. If that's the case, players in these markets might find the game appears in their stores in the coming days without any official explanation beyond "it's there now."

There is, of course, the possibility that GTA VI will not be available in one or more of these markets at all. That outcome would likely require an announcement from either Rockstar, Sony, or a relevant regulatory authority. None has been made. Treating that outcome as confirmed before any announcement exists isn't accurate reporting.

Saudi Arabia and UAE Are a Different Story

One detail worth paying attention to: Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates are not on Sony's exclusion list for the GTA+ promotional bonus. That means players in those two Gulf countries are eligible for the free month if they pre-order digitally, which also suggests the game is available in those storefronts without the same complications affecting the other four.

That inconsistency matters. If GTA VI were being restricted across the Gulf region as a bloc, you'd expect Saudi Arabia and the UAE to be part of that picture. The fact that they're apparently not suggests the situation is specific to Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman, rather than a broad Middle East exclusion. Whether the reason is regulatory, administrative, or something else entirely, the pattern doesn't fit a simple regional sweep.

GTA VI Has a History of Regional Complications

This isn't the first time a Rockstar title has run into regional availability questions. The GTA series in particular has faced scrutiny in a number of markets over the years due to its content, and Gulf countries with active content rating and approval systems have a history of assessing games carefully before permitting sale.

That doesn't mean GTA VI will be restricted in these markets. It means the game isn't arriving in a vacuum, and the absence of a store listing isn't necessarily a surprise when you factor in how different the approval process can look from one country to the next. A game that goes live for pre-order in the US and UK on day one might legitimately take longer to appear in markets with separate regulatory requirements.

It's also worth keeping in mind that players in these regions who want to purchase the game have options beyond a local PlayStation Store. Region-switching and physical copies from neighbouring markets are both things people do when a specific storefront doesn't carry a title. Whether that becomes necessary here is genuinely unclear right now.

No Statement From Rockstar or Sony

As of publication, neither Rockstar Games nor Sony Interactive Entertainment has addressed the missing GTA VI listings in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait or Oman. There has been no press release, no community post, and no update to Sony's FAQ that explains the situation for players in those regions.

Players who have reached out through social media haven't received any response clarifying whether the game will be available to them and when. That silence is frustrating if you're in one of the affected markets and trying to figure out whether to look for an alternative, but it doesn't confirm anything either way.

Rockstar's official channels and Sony's support pages are the places to watch if any statement does come. Until one does, the current situation is exactly what it looks like from the outside: four PlayStation storefronts where GTA VI doesn't appear, with no official explanation on the table.

For now, the most accurate description of what's happening is that four Gulf storefronts are missing a listing that most of the world can already access, and nobody has said why.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GTA 6 banned in Qatar?

No official ban has been confirmed by Rockstar Games, Sony Interactive Entertainment or Qatari authorities. The game is currently absent from Qatar's PlayStation Store search results, but the reason has not been explained publicly.

Why can't I find GTA 6 on the PlayStation Store in Bahrain, Kuwait or Oman?

GTA VI does not appear in PlayStation Store search results for Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman as of this writing. Sony has not provided an explanation. Possible reasons include pending content approvals, publishing delays, or regional regulatory reviews that have not yet concluded.

Does Sony's FAQ say GTA 6 is banned in these countries?

No. Sony's FAQ only states that Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman are excluded from the GTA+ free-month promotional offer tied to digital pre-orders. It makes no claim that the game itself is banned or permanently unavailable.

Are Saudi Arabia and UAE affected?

Saudi Arabia and the UAE are not on Sony's GTA+ promotion exclusion list, which suggests those storefronts are not experiencing the same situation as Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman.

Will GTA 6 ever be available in Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Oman?

Unknown at this stage. No official restriction has been announced. Respawnerz will update this article if the situation changes.
