Everything Announced at the VR Games Showcase Summer 2026

Two new extraction shooters, two games that shadow-dropped mid-show, a Disney DLC pack for a trombone game, and a heist title getting its own Direct right after the credits. Here is every trailer from the sixth VR Games Showcase, main show and pre-show.

June 24, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 9 min read

VR Games Showcase wrapped its sixth show on June 23, and if you stepped away from your headset for two hours, you missed a real pile of news. Twenty-five trailers in the main show, eight more in the pre-show, two surprise day-of launches, and a heist game that got its own dedicated Direct the moment the credits rolled. Here is everything that came out of it.

Two New Extraction Shooters Want Your Squad

Triangle Factory used its slot to reveal Breachers: Outbreak, a spin-off of its Breachers universe that turns the series' tactical gunplay loose on a zombie outbreak instead of human enemies. It is a 4-player co-op extraction shooter where you and your friends drop into infected quarantine zones to recover the key to stopping an undead apocalypse, coming to Meta Quest, Pico, SteamVR, and Steam sometime in 2026.

Salmi Games picked a similar lane with DRIFTERS: Blackout Crew, a PvE extraction shooter where up to four players raid ancient alien temples to steal energy for a dying mothership. Loot from each run turns into weapon parts and suit upgrades back on the ship, the usual extraction loop, but built around tactile VR gunplay. No release date yet, just Quest and SteamVR confirmed.

Two Games Launched the Same Day They Were Shown

1A3M Studio shadow-dropped Skeleton Crew on Quest 3 and 3S the moment the showcase ended. It is a physics-driven pirate co-op game where you and a friend slash, steal, and battle your way through "Pirate Purgatory," collecting severed skulls as trophies along the way. Dirty Pearl Studios did the same with INWIGO, a cooperative horror extraction game about kids finding things hidden in a haunted manor that adults cannot see, also live on Quest in early access as of showcase day.

FunkyMouse chose the same window to flip the switch on VR support for motorbike racer Lanesplit, now playable on SteamVR and Steam with new thunderstorm weather, redlined electric bikes, and three real motorcycle influencers as unlockable riders.

The Big Names Got Updates, Not New Games

Fast Travel Games used its slot for a developer diary on Payday: Aces High rather than fresh footage, then ran a dedicated Payday: Aces High Direct right after the main show for anyone who wanted more on the 4-player VR heist game. RUST showed a melee combat featurette for Hot Dogs, Horseshoes & Hand Grenades 2 without giving the sequel a release date. 1C Game Studios confirmed Korea. IL-2 Series enters PC and PC VR early access on June 25, putting players in the cockpits of early jet fighters during the Korean War.

Flat2VR Studios revealed that Trombone Champ: Unflattened is getting a full Disney DLC pack this summer called Movie Magic Volume 1: six songs pulled from Aladdin, The Little Mermaid, Toy Story, Beauty and the Beast, and The Lion King, plus two new themed performance venues. FlatOut 4: Total Insanity VR stays in SteamVR early access, but its PlayStation Store page is now live for PS VR2 wishlisting. VRacer Hoverbike picked up new paid track DLC and a weekly multiplayer meetup, and Maestro, the hand-tracked orchestra conducting game, is getting an Attack on Titan crossover.

A Long List of New Games to Wishlist

Past the headline reveals, the show moved fast through more than a dozen smaller announcements. DreamVR's The Rifted Skies is a parkour roguelite about outrunning a collapsing world, due this fall on Quest and SteamVR. Polarity One's co-op shooter Exoshock adds multiplayer in July. Squido had a packed show, revealing a town-builder (Outlanders VR), a free-to-play pet collector (Nomsters), and a dragon flight game (Roar), on top of a Wizherd update and a closer look at The Lightkeepers, which lands as a Quest exclusive on September 10.

Meta Quest also got a firm date for Transformers: Beyond Reality Redux, an on-rails shooter built around a fictional fusion of Cybertronian and human tech, arriving September 3. Singular Perception locked in Sol Protocol for September on Quest and October on PC VR. FusionPlay's R.A.I.D. mixes cargo inspection with co-op gunfights aboard a space station, arriving this fall. Play XD's mixed-reality card battler Primal Rumble opens its closed beta this summer. VirtualAge showed new footage of squad shooter Guardians Planetfall, still pegged for "Q4" without a firmer date, and Flat2VR Spark confirmed I Am Your Beast VR, a full VR port of the acclaimed revenge-thriller shooter, for SteamVR early access later this year.

The Pre-Show Wasn't Just Filler

The fifteen-minute countdown show had its own lineup worth catching. Studio CyFi's Bullet Time Agent is a slow-time bullet hell platformer headed to Quest and PC. Salmon Man went multiplayer in a July update. Fixer Undercover, an escape-room game about going undercover as a maintenance worker in a corrupt prison, gets its SteamVR release on July 16. Bamboo Grove leans into VR horror's favorite trick right now, putting you in the role of a game tester whose horror title starts bleeding into reality, due in September.

Beat the Beats added a Steam Workshop level editor the same day, Augmental Puzzles launched a mixed-reality nonograms mode, and Paracosm Studio's Dagger Woods VR and Vision Bridge's Daumier: Lila & Ghost both got fresh trailers, the former a Nova Scotia folklore thriller, the latter a cooperative puzzle game set inside a gallery of living artworks.

What This Show Says About VR Right Now

Nothing here is going to sell a headset to someone who wasn't already shopping for one. There is no Half-Life: Alyx moment, and a few of these are reskins or version bumps rather than new ideas. But more than thirty announcements spread across horror, racing, shooters, and cozy sims, most of them targeting two or three platforms at once, is a decent sign for a platform that spent the last few months absorbing rough headlines, Rec Room's shutdown among them. This was VR's second major showcase of the summer after UploadVR's event on June 12, and the volume coming out of both says the developers building for headsets are not slowing down, even with platform holders funding fewer big exclusives.

Full replays of the main show and pre-show are up on the VR Games Showcase YouTube channel, and the Payday: Aces High Direct is there too if heists are your thing.
