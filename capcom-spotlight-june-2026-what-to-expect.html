Everything to Expect From Tonight's Capcom Spotlight

Dragon's Dogma II gets a Dark Arisen expansion, Onimusha may be moving its release date, and fans are still asking about Resident Evil Veronica even though Capcom has not given them a single reason to. Here is the full picture before the stream goes live.

June 25, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 7 min read

Capcom's next Spotlight broadcast goes live tonight, and while the publisher has been unusually upfront about what is actually on the menu, there is still real news to chase here. A Switch 2 port confirmation, a possible release date shuffle to clear a crowded September window, and one question fans will not stop asking even though Capcom has not given them a single reason to.

Here is everything confirmed, everything rumored, and what to actually watch for when the stream starts.

When and Where to Watch

The broadcast goes live tonight at the following times:

  • 2:00 PM PDT / 5:00 PM EDT / 10:00 PM BST
  • 2:30 AM IST (June 26) / 7:00 AM JST (June 26)

It streams live on Capcom's official Twitch and YouTube channels, with a simultaneous TikTok simulcast. Subtitles are available in 13 languages. Runtime is roughly 30 minutes, and Capcom has flagged that the broadcast includes footage from age-restricted titles.

One thing worth setting expectations on before you tune in: Capcom has explicitly billed this as a focused round-up, not a surprise-heavy event. The publisher confirmed three headlining games going in, and by their own framing, what you see in the preview is close to what you are getting. Do not walk in expecting a mystery fourth title to reshape the whole night. Though more on that in a moment.

The Three Confirmed Games

Dragon's Dogma II: Dark Arisen

The biggest concrete news of the night is already locked in before the stream even starts. Dragon's Dogma II is getting a Dark Arisen-style expansion with a new region, new mechanics, and a confirmed October 9 launch date. The Switch 2 port is the headline detail. Capcom has been steadily migrating its RE Engine catalog to Nintendo's new hardware all year, and Dragon's Dogma II joining that list is a real statement. This is not just remasters and spin-offs anymore. The platform is becoming a serious home for Capcom's bigger action-RPGs.

Tonight's segment will almost certainly focus on what the expansion actually adds rather than re-covering the base game. Watch for specifics on new vocations or mechanic changes, a first look at the new region's biome and enemy design, and if Capcom is feeling generous, some performance details on how a game this dense handles on Switch 2 hardware.

Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection

Stories 3 launched back in March to a solid reception, so this segment is not about a new reveal. It is about life after launch. Expect a deeper look at the rumored Rudy story expansion and details on the "Royal monsters" endgame content Capcom has been hinting at for players who already finished the main campaign. If you put time into Stories 3 and want to know whether to come back, this is the segment that actually answers that question.

Onimusha: Way of the Sword

This is the segment with the most potential for live news. Way of the Sword has a playable demo already live across platforms and a confirmed September 25 release date. The problem is that date puts it directly next to two other major launches: Remedy's Control Resonant and Konami's Silent Hill: Townfall, both landing September 24. Industry chatter, including signals from at least one retailer listing, points to Capcom potentially pulling the date forward to September 4 instead.

If that date change gets announced tonight, that is the headline of the broadcast. Not because a few weeks on the calendar matter on their own, but because it tells you something about how seriously publishers are now planning around each other heading into a genuinely stacked back half of the year. Beyond the date question, expect new gameplay footage around the game's single-katana combat focus and a closer look at the "wide linear" level design Capcom has mentioned in recent interviews.

At a Glance: What Is Confirmed Tonight

Game What to Expect Key Date
Dragon's Dogma II: Dark Arisen New region, new mechanics, Switch 2 port confirmed October 9
Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection Post-launch content, Rudy expansion, Royal monsters TBA 2026
Onimusha: Way of the Sword New gameplay footage, possible date change from Sep 25 Sep 25 (may move to Sep 4)

The Wildcard Nobody Can Let Go Of

Capcom has been quietly converting big chunks of its RE Engine library to Switch 2 throughout the year. That has fed a persistent fan expectation that tonight could be the night for a Resident Evil Veronica remake reveal, or a Switch 2 announcement for the RE2, RE3, and RE4 remake trilogy.

To be clear: Capcom has not signaled either of those is coming tonight. By the publisher's own description, this Spotlight is built around the three games above. Resident Evil Veronica and the existing remake trilogy have specifically been left off the announced lineup.

That has not stopped the speculation. And it is worth paying attention to for one real reason: Capcom has a track record of confirming Switch 2 ports for its back catalog with minimal lead time, often slipping them into broadcasts that were already billed as something else entirely. It would be a genuine surprise rather than an expected beat. But if you are the type of viewer setting an alarm for a 2:30 AM stream, this is probably what got you out of bed. No judgment.

The Three Things Worth Tracking Live

Strip away the noise and there are really three questions this broadcast is going to answer, or not answer:

Does the Onimusha release date move? This is the most likely actual news of the night and has real ripple effects across the entire September calendar. If it moves, every other publisher with a September window is going to notice.

What does Dragon's Dogma II: Dark Arisen actually add? "New region and mechanics" is doing a lot of work in Capcom's own description. The specifics matter for anyone trying to decide whether to buy back in, especially on Switch 2.

Does Capcom say anything at all about Resident Evil Veronica or the remake trilogy on Switch 2? Even a throwaway "coming later this year" would be the biggest moment of the broadcast by a wide margin. Expectations have been lowered enough that any signal at all would land hard.

Capcom Spotlight runs roughly 30 minutes starting tonight. Indian viewers, that is 2:30 AM IST on June 26. Set the alarm, or check back here right after the broadcast wraps for a full recap of everything announced.
