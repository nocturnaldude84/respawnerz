GTA VI Second Trailer Drops: Everything We Spotted in 4 Minutes

Rockstar just dropped the second trailer and we broke it down frame by frame. Here's what actually matters.

Jun 07, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 5 min read

Rockstar dropped the second GTA VI trailer without warning at midnight ET on Thursday, and the internet predictably collapsed. Four minutes and twelve seconds. That's all we got. But those four minutes contain more detail than most games' full reveal events, so here's everything worth talking about.

The Setting Looks Bigger Than Expected

The original trailer gave us Vice City's beachfront strip and neon signs. This one pulls back the camera. We're seeing highways cutting through what looks like the Everglades, swamp towns, and at one point what appears to be an inland city with a completely different visual identity from the coast. This is not a one-city map.

The lighting work is extraordinary. The golden hour shots in the swamp areas have a level of environmental detail that feels genuinely next-generation. Rockstar has clearly been sitting on this engine capability for a while.

"The scale of what Rockstar is showing suggests a map that makes GTA V's Los Santos feel like a warmup exercise."

Lucia Gets More Screen Time

The female protagonist from the first trailer returns and we get more context. She's seen in what appears to be a probation office at the start of the trailer, which matches earlier leaks about her backstory. The male co-protagonist appears around the one-minute mark, and their dynamic in the brief scenes together suggests this is a proper dual-narrative structure rather than a switchable character system like GTA V.

The Mission Footage

Two mission sequences are shown. One involves a high-speed boat chase through what looks like open water near the coast, and the level of water physics detail is genuinely stunning. The second is a heist setup in a location that resembles an art museum. Both sequences appear to be from early in the game based on the character dialogue.

What the Trailer Confirms

A few things are now locked in based on what we can see. First, this is set in the present day. Signs, phones, and cultural references in the background all confirm contemporary setting. Second, the wanted system appears to have been significantly redesigned. Third, and this is interesting, there are at least two different law enforcement agencies visible, suggesting a more layered legal system than previous titles.

Release Window

The trailer closes with "2026" and nothing else. Given Rockstar's history of holding firm on dates, that's a real window. Whether it's a fall launch or a holiday push is still unknown.
