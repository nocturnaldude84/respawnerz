CDL Major IV Day 1 Live: Miami Heretics Complete Reverse Sweep, Toronto Ultra KOI Dominate Elimination Bracket

Day one of CDL Major IV delivered everything from dominant sweeps to dramatic comebacks. Miami Heretics rallied from 0-2 down to eliminate Carolina Royal Ravens, Toronto Ultra KOI swept Boston Breach after knocking out Riyadh Falcons earlier in the day, and the tournament now heads into Saturday's action in Paris.

June 26, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 10 min read

Day one of CDL Major IV has produced no shortage of drama in Paris, with the elimination bracket delivering two more memorable finishes. Miami Heretics completed a stunning reverse sweep to knock out the Carolina Royal Ravens after trailing 2-0, while Toronto Ultra KOI continued their remarkable run through the lower bracket with a commanding 3-0 sweep of Boston Breach. With another round of eliminations complete, attention now turns to the remaining matches as Major IV rolls into its next stage.

Major IV is the last LAN of the regular CDL season before everyone packs up for Champs in Las Vegas, and the bracket reflects that. All 12 franchises made the trip, hosted this time by the Gentle Mates on home turf, and there is $365,000 on the table with the winner walking away with $150,000 and 100 CDL Points. Every series is a best of five until the grand final, which goes to a best of seven. Nobody wants to be the team that gets bounced early here, because the points on offer are some of the last chances for bubble teams to lock in a Champs seed.

OpTic Texas def. Boston Breach, 3-1

OpTic opened their Major IV campaign the way they have opened most of this season: fast, and without much patience for a back-and-forth series. They took Hacienda Hardpoint 250-141, dropped Hacienda Search and Destroy 2-6 to Breach's only map win of the day, then closed it out by taking Den Overload 5-3 and Sake Hardpoint 250-208. The series never got to a fifth map.

The map veto told its own story before a shot was fired. OpTic banned Gridlock Hardpoint, Fringe Search and Destroy, and Gridlock Overload. Breach countered with Colossus, Sake, and Exposure off the table, which meant Sake stayed live for OpTic and ended up being the map that finished the job.

That final map on Sake ran 9 minutes and 49 seconds and finished 250-208, and the individual numbers explain why Breach never got the hill control they needed. Huke led OpTic at 30/18 with 3,580 damage and a team-high nine objective kills, and Shotzzy backed him up with 29/22 and a series-best 25 assists, which is the kind of number that points to a team playing around the slayer rather than freelancing. Dashy chipped in 21/22 with 11 assists and 347 damage per hill rotation. Mercules had the quietest box score of the four at 14/21, but he still picked up six objective kills and the team's only zero in contested hill time, meaning nobody was taking the hill away from him when he had it.

Boston Breach were not run over on that final map, for what it's worth. Purj actually out-damaged everyone on the server with 3,662 across the map and finished 29/22, and his 2.88 kills per hill and 365.88 damage per hill were both better numbers than three of OpTic's four players. The problem was the support around him. Tjhaly went 18/23, Afro went 19/27, and Spart went 17/24 with the team's best six objective kills, but Breach's deaths kept piling up faster than OpTic's, and a Hardpoint where your star player has to outscore the entire other roster by himself is not a Hardpoint you tend to win.

It's a clean 3-1 for OpTic and a trip straight into winners round 2, while Boston Breach drop down to the elimination side of the bracket, where one more loss ends their Major.

G2 Minnesota def. Riyadh Falcons

G2 Minnesota punched their ticket to Winners Round 2 after holding off a determined Riyadh Falcons comeback in one of the closest series of CDL Major IV's opening day. G2 looked in complete control early, taking Colossus Hardpoint 250-204 before dominating Hacienda Search and Destroy 6-1 to move within a single map of closing out the series.

Riyadh Falcons refused to go quietly. They stayed alive with a 3-1 victory on Scar Overload before edging out G2 250-215 on Scar Hardpoint, forcing a decisive fifth map after trailing 2-0.

With the series on the line, G2 regrouped on Den Search and Destroy and delivered when it mattered most. They secured a 6-4 victory to stop the Falcons' reverse sweep attempt and seal a hard-fought 3-2 series win.

With OpTic Texas and G2 Minnesota safely through to Winners Round 2, the remaining winners bracket matches are now taking center stage in Paris. FaZe Vegas currently hold a 2-1 advantage over hosts Paris Gentle Mates, while the Los Angeles Thieves have surged to a 2-0 lead against the Carolina Royal Ravens. The action is far from over at CDL Major IV. FaZe Vegas sit one map away from eliminating hosts Paris Gentle Mates after edging ahead 2-1, while the Los Angeles Thieves have put themselves in a dominant position against the Carolina Royal Ravens with back-to-back wins on Den Hardpoint and Fringe Search and Destroy. We'll continue updating this live blog with every result, bracket update, and key moment as the opening day unfolds.

Los Angeles Thieves def. Carolina Royal Ravens, 3-0

The Los Angeles Thieves wasted little time securing their place in Winners Round 2, sweeping the Carolina Royal Ravens in one of the most one-sided series of the opening round. LA controlled the pace from the very first map, cruising to a 250-129 victory on Den Hardpoint before extending their lead with a convincing 6-4 win on Fringe Search and Destroy.

Any hopes of a Carolina comeback were quickly extinguished in Overload Control. The Thieves delivered a flawless performance, shutting out the Royal Ravens 8-0 to complete the sweep in emphatic fashion. It was a statement victory from a team that looked comfortable in every mode, rarely allowing Carolina to build any momentum throughout the series.

With the 3-0 win, the Los Angeles Thieves join OpTic Texas, G2 Minnesota, and FaZe Vegas in Winners Round 2. Carolina Royal Ravens now fall into the elimination bracket, where one more defeat will end their Major IV campaign.

Toronto Ultra KOI def. Riyadh Falcons, 3-0

Toronto Ultra KOI kept their CDL Major IV campaign alive with an emphatic 3-0 sweep over the Riyadh Falcons in the elimination bracket. KOI set the tone from the opening map, controlling Colossus Hardpoint for a comfortable 250-159 victory before carrying that momentum into Fringe Search and Destroy, where they secured a convincing 6-3 win to move within one map of advancing.

The Falcons needed a response in Exposure Control but were unable to mount a comeback. Toronto Ultra KOI stayed in control throughout the third map, closing out a 4-2 victory to complete the sweep and eliminate Riyadh from the tournament. It was a composed performance from KOI, who looked sharp across all three game modes and never allowed the Falcons to build sustained momentum.

With the 3-0 victory, Toronto Ultra KOI move on to the next round of the elimination bracket and keep their Major IV hopes alive. Riyadh Falcons, meanwhile, become the first team eliminated after falling in both the winners bracket and the lower bracket on day one in Paris.

Boston Breach def. Vancouver Surge, 3-2

Boston Breach survived a major scare to keep their CDL Major IV campaign alive, defeating the Vancouver Surge 3-2 in one of the tournament's most entertaining elimination matches. Breach looked to be cruising after taking Den Hardpoint 250-218 and Raid Search and Destroy 6-3 to build a commanding two-map lead.

Vancouver refused to go quietly. The Surge clawed their way back into the series with a narrow 3-2 victory on Den Control before dominating Scar Hardpoint 250-210 to complete the comeback and force a winner-take-all fifth map.

With elimination on the line, Boston Breach regrouped when it mattered most. They delivered a composed performance on Hacienda Search and Destroy, defeating the Surge 6-1 to close out the series and advance in the lower bracket. Vancouver Surge's Major IV campaign comes to an end, while Breach move on to face another day in Paris.

Paris Gentle Mates def. Cloud9 New York, 3-0

Paris Gentle Mates kept their CDL Major IV campaign alive with an emphatic 3-0 sweep over Cloud9 New York in the elimination bracket. Playing in front of their home crowd, the hosts wasted no time taking control of the series, dismantling Cloud9 250-51 on Sake Hardpoint before edging out a tense 6-5 victory on Den Search and Destroy to move within one map of advancing.

Any hopes of a Cloud9 comeback were quickly shut down in Exposure Control. Paris carried their momentum into the third map, producing another dominant display to secure a convincing 6-1 victory and complete the sweep. Despite the close Search and Destroy, Gentle Mates controlled the series from start to finish and never allowed Cloud9 to seize the momentum.

The victory keeps Paris Gentle Mates alive at Major IV, where they continue their lower bracket run in front of the home fans. Cloud9 New York, meanwhile, see their tournament come to an end after falling in straight maps.

Miami Heretics def. Carolina Royal Ravens, 3-2

Miami Heretics completed one of the biggest comebacks of CDL Major IV so far, recovering from a 2-0 series deficit to eliminate the Carolina Royal Ravens in dramatic fashion. Carolina looked to be cruising after edging Colossus Hardpoint 250-240 and following it up with a comfortable 6-2 victory on Hacienda Search and Destroy to move within one map of advancing.

The momentum shifted completely on Scar Control. Miami responded with a dominant 6-2 win before carrying that confidence into Hacienda Hardpoint, where they overwhelmed the Ravens 250-120 to level the series and force a deciding fifth map.

With elimination on the line, the Heretics delivered when it mattered most. Miami closed out the comeback with a convincing 6-3 victory on Raid Search and Destroy, completing the reverse sweep to advance in the lower bracket. Carolina Royal Ravens, meanwhile, bow out of Major IV after letting a two-map advantage slip away.

Toronto Ultra KOI def. Boston Breach, 3-0

Toronto Ultra KOI continued their impressive lower bracket run with a clinical 3-0 sweep over Boston Breach in Elimination Round 2. Fresh off eliminating Riyadh Falcons earlier in the day, KOI carried that momentum into another dominant performance, taking Sake Hardpoint 250-165 before extending their lead with a 6-3 victory on Fringe Search and Destroy.

Boston Breach never managed to mount a response. KOI wrapped up the series on Scar Control with a composed 4-2 victory, completing the sweep without dropping a map and booking their place in the next round of the lower bracket.

With two straight sweeps on the day, Toronto Ultra KOI have firmly established themselves as one of the hottest teams remaining in the elimination bracket, while Boston Breach's Major IV campaign comes to an end.

The lower bracket ended the day with two contrasting stories. Miami Heretics pulled off one of the tournament's biggest comebacks, winning three straight maps to eliminate Carolina Royal Ravens after falling behind 2-0. Toronto Ultra KOI, meanwhile, never gave Boston Breach a chance, sweeping the series 3-0 to secure back-to-back elimination victories in a single day. What's Next at CDL Major IV

Action resumes on Saturday, June 27, with three more elimination bracket matchups set to decide who keeps their Major IV hopes alive. Miami Heretics will take on Paris Gentle Mates, G2 Minnesota face OpTic Texas in a blockbuster showdown, and Los Angeles Thieves meet FaZe Vegas to round out the day's schedule.

  • Miami Heretics vs. Paris Gentle Mates – 5:30 PM IST
  • G2 Minnesota vs. OpTic Texas – 7:00 PM IST
  • Los Angeles Thieves vs. FaZe Vegas – 8:30 PM IST

Toronto KOI have also secured their place in Saturday's schedule after sweeping Boston Breach, but their opponent will be determined once the remaining elimination bracket results are finalized.
