Evo 2026 Day 1 Recap: Street Fighter 6, Tekken 8, and 2XKO Steal the Floor

Day one is in the books at Evo Las Vegas 2026, and between pool play upsets, publisher panels, and a show floor packed wall to wall, there's already plenty to unpack before the real damage gets done this weekend.

June 27, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 6 min read

Evo Las Vegas 2026 opened the way it always does: a sea of pools, a handful of early shocks, and just enough side news to keep everyone's second screen lit up between matches. Saturday and Sunday are when the brackets actually get dangerous, but day one already gave fans plenty to argue about.

Here's everything that happened on day one, and what's worth watching as the weekend tightens up.

Street Fighter 6 Survives Its Biggest Bracket Yet

Street Fighter 6 pulled one of the largest fields in Evo history again, with thousands of entrants working through pools all day. Most of the scene's top names came through clean, but a few early upsets already have people rewinding clips. The kind of results that get clipped and passed around Discord within minutes of happening. Finals are locked in for Sunday, and the bracket is shaping up to be one of the messier ones in recent memory.

Tekken 8 Pools Open With Legends on the Mat

Tekken 8's opening pools brought out a real who's who: Arslan Ash, Knee, JDCR, LowHigh, Atif, all grinding through one of the largest open brackets at the event. Day one in Tekken isn't really about highlight reels. It's about survival. The real fireworks tend to show up once the field thins out, so the story right now is simply who's still standing heading into Saturday.

2XKO Is the Game Everyone's Lining Up For

Riot's tag fighter has turned into one of the busiest spots on the show floor. Outside of its own tournament bracket, attendees are getting hands-on time with the latest build, and the line for it has reportedly stretched well past the booths on either side. For a game still working toward a full release, that kind of foot traffic at its first major LAN test is exactly the read Riot was hoping for.

Publisher Panels Bring the Reveals

Several publishers have presentations scheduled across the weekend, including Capcom, Bandai Namco, SNK, Arc System Works, and Riot Games. Expect the usual mix of DLC character reveals, balance updates, roadmap talk, new trailers, and at least one announcement nobody saw coming. Arc System Works already got a head start with its own Showcase on June 24, where it detailed new titles including the tactical RPG Qliphah in Providence's Shadow and confirmed Absolum is heading to Switch 2 on September 17.

Invincible VS Gets Its Moment

One of the newer additions to the Evo lineup, Invincible VS, is both playable on the floor and running its own dedicated tournament. It's a smaller stage than Street Fighter or Tekken, obviously, but it puts the game in front of a crowd that knows exactly what to look for. The first DLC characters, Immortal and Universa, join the roster on June 30 alongside the start of Season 1, so this weekend doubles as a preview of what's coming next.

The Show Floor Is Its Own Event

Strip away the brackets entirely and Evo is still one of the biggest weekends on the fighting game calendar just for the floor. Hundreds of arcade cabinets. Indie fighting game showcases. Artist Alley, merch vendors, community-run side tournaments, and enough cosplay to fill a week's worth of feeds. For a lot of attendees, this is the actual draw, brackets or no brackets.

What to Watch This Weekend

The real news at Evo almost always lands Saturday and Sunday, not day one. Keep an eye out for new DLC character announcements, fresh gameplay trailers, release date confirmations, and the Grand Finals for the event's biggest titles. Publishers have a habit of holding their best cards for the main stage, so don't be shocked if something nobody predicted shows up before the weekend's out.

Check back as pools wrap and the brackets tighten heading into Sunday's finals.
