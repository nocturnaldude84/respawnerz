No, Xbox Hasn't Closed Ninja Theory Yet - Here's the Actual Status of Every Xbox Studio Reportedly at Risk

Jason Schreier stepped in to correct the record this week. The "Ninja Theory is already dead" headlines have been wrong for two weeks now, and somehow they're still circulating. Here's what's actually confirmed.

June 27, 2026 by Respawnerz Staff 6 min read

If you've seen a headline this week claiming Xbox already shut down Ninja Theory, it's wrong, and it's been wrong since the day it first ran. Bloomberg's Jason Schreier, the reporter whose name is actually attached to this story, went on Reddit's r/Games this week specifically to say so.

That a journalist had to personally show up in a comment section to correct his own reporting tells you everything about how this rumor cycle has gone. So let's actually sort out what's confirmed, what's still a rumor, and what's genuinely just in limbo, because right now those three categories are getting flattened into one big "it's over" headline that isn't true.

Where This Started

Go back to June 15. Schreier reported that several Xbox studios were in active negotiations to spin off as a way to avoid closure, as part of Microsoft's broader cost-cutting efforts. He named Compulsion Games and Double Fine specifically, and said Ninja Theory was "also in conversations," along with unnamed others described as being "at risk."

Somewhere between that report and the headlines that followed, "in conversations" and "at risk" turned into "closing." Subsequent reports and social media discussions interpreted the situation as though Ninja Theory had already been shut down. That's the version that continues to circulate online, even though later clarifications have shown the situation is more nuanced.

What Schreier Actually Said This Week

Schreier went back on r/Games to correct a thread that had, in his words, an incorrect title claiming the closures were done deals. His point was simple: Xbox has not closed Ninja Theory, at least not as of now. Reports claiming the studio has already been shut down are inaccurate, and Microsoft has not announced any closure. He did confirm that Compulsion Games' management has given staff permission to start job hunting, which is a real sign of where that one is headed, but a studio quietly bracing for bad news is not the same thing as a studio that's already closed.

That distinction matters more than it sounds like it should. "Bracing for layoffs" and "shut down" describe very different situations, but the two have frequently been conflated as the story has spread.

Status Check: Every Studio Actually in Question

Studio Status as of June 27 What We Actually Know
Ninja Theory (Hellblade) Not closed Schreier says reports claiming Xbox has already closed Ninja Theory are inaccurate. No closure has been announced.
Compulsion Games (South of Midnight) Future uncertain Staff given permission to job hunt. Some departures already posted on LinkedIn.
Double Fine (Psychonauts, Keeper) Reportedly exploring a spinoff Reportedly negotiating to go independent. Reports suggest some job losses could occur even if a spinoff happens.

The pattern is clear. None of these studios has been officially confirmed as closed. Everything here is still being negotiated behind closed doors, which is exactly why nobody outside Microsoft can responsibly call it one way or the other yet.

The Nine-Day Problem Nobody's Talking About

Here's the detail that makes this whole situation feel worse than a normal rumor mix-up. Ninja Theory stood on stage at the Xbox Games Showcase on June 7 and revealed Senua, a third Hellblade game. Nine days later, the closure reports broke. Whether or not Ninja Theory ends up surviving this, that timing alone is rough. You don't put a studio in the spotlight with a new franchise entry and then have it become the center of an "is this studio already dead?" news cycle barely a week and a half later.

Some have floated the idea that Microsoft featured Senua specifically to make Ninja Theory a more attractive sale to an outside buyer. There is no evidence Microsoft featured the game for that reason, and the theory remains speculative.

Why the Confusion Keeps Spreading

Part of the confusion comes from the fact that "Xbox closes beloved Hellblade studio" is a much simpler headline than "Xbox studio remains in unresolved negotiations that could end in layoffs, an independent spinoff, or possibly no closure at all." The second version is the accurate one. Early reports and social media posts spread rapidly, and later clarifications have not received the same level of attention.

The other part is that this genuinely is a fluid situation. Microsoft hasn't put out any public statement confirming or denying specific studio outcomes, which leaves a vacuum that gets filled with whatever the loudest version of the rumor happens to be that week.

What to Actually Watch For

If you want to know when this is actually resolved instead of just rumored, watch for an official statement from Microsoft, not another anonymous source claiming to know the outcome. Watch Compulsion Games specifically, since staff there have already been told to start job hunting. And if Ninja Theory does get spun off or sold rather than closed outright, expect that to come with its own announcement, likely timed well clear of any Xbox showcase.

Until Microsoft makes an official announcement, nobody outside those negotiations knows how this ends. That's a less satisfying headline than "it's over." It also happens to be the most accurate answer available today.
